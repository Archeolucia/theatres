Introduction

The GIS here proposed is the result of the project “circuses, theaters, amphitheaters: where, when and why” aimed to collect data about entertainment buildings all over the roman empire. First step of the work was study of the published works, especially the ones by J. H. Humphrey (1986) and R. G. Chase (2002), and the historical sources.

Afterwards the building have been mapped thanks to the identification through satellite images. Four different categories of buildings have been detected: circuses, theaters, amphitheaters and stadiums.

For each category different informations are available: name, city, nation, if the building is exactly located and if it is still visible today. The circuses are the main focus of this research and, for this typology, the following information is available: year of construction, century of construction, size (meters), if the circus is linked to an imperial palace and the minimum distance from the city walls (meters).
